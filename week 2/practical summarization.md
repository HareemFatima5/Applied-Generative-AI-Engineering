# Practical Task — Summarization

## What is it?
This is the practical application of summarization prompting. Here I combined summarization with other techniques like output constraints and audience targeting to get more useful and controlled outputs.

## Examples I Tried

### Example 1 — Summarization with Output Constraints
**Prompt:**
Summarize the text below in 3 bullet points.
Each bullet must be one sentence only.
No introduction or conclusion.

Text: Scientists have discovered a new species of deep sea fish near the 
coast of New Zealand. The fish was found at a depth of 3000 meters and 
has no eyes since it lives in complete darkness. Researchers say this 
discovery helps us better understand how life adapts to extreme environments.

**Output I got:**
* Scientists have identified a new species of deep-sea fish off the coast of New Zealand.
* The creature thrives in total darkness at a depth of 3000 meters and lacks functional eyes.
* This discovery provides valuable insights into how organisms adapt to extreme environments.

### Example 2 — Summarization for a Specific Audience
**Prompt:**
Summarize the text below in 2 sentences for a student who has never 
heard of Python before. Keep it very simple.

Text: The Python programming language was created by Guido van Rossum 
and first released in 1991. It is known for its simple and readable 
syntax which makes it beginner friendly. Python is widely used in web 
development, data science, artificial intelligence, and automation. 
It has a large community and thousands of free libraries available.

**Output I got:**
Python is a popular, easy-to-read programming language that is designed 
to be simple for beginners to learn. It is used by people all over the 
world to build websites, work with data, and create artificial intelligence.

### Example 3 — Summarization with Word Limit
**Prompt:**
Summarize the text below in exactly 30 words, not more not less.

Text: Meditation has been practiced for thousands of years and has many 
proven health benefits. Regular meditation can reduce stress and anxiety, 
improve focus and concentration and even lower blood pressure. Studies 
show that just 10 minutes of daily meditation can lead to noticeable 
improvements in mental health over time.

**Output I got:**
Practicing meditation for thousands of years provides numerous health 
advantages. Ten minutes daily reduces stress, enhances focus, and lowers 
blood pressure, leading to significant long-term improvements in your 
mental wellbeing.
